Fresh Vegetables

Supermarkets typically offer a wide variety of fresh vegetables, including leafy greens like spinach, kale, and lettuce, root vegetables such as potatoes, carrots, and beets, cruciferous vegetables like broccoli, cauliflower, and cabbage, as well as tomatoes, cucumbers, bell peppers, onions, garlic, and more. Fresh vegetables are versatile ingredients used in salads, stir-fries, soups, stews, and numerous other dishes.

Frozen Vegetables

Frozen vegetables are convenient options that have been harvested at peak ripeness and quickly frozen to retain their nutrients and flavor. They come in a variety of options, including mixed vegetables, peas, corn, green beans, broccoli florets, and more. Frozen vegetables are often used as side dishes, added to casseroles, stir-fries, or soups, or incorporated into smoothies.

Fresh-Cut Vegetables

Supermarkets often provide pre-cut and pre-packaged fresh vegetables, such as carrot sticks, celery sticks, bell pepper strips, and salad mixes. These convenient options are ready to eat and can be used for snacking, dipping, or as salad ingredients.

OUR VENUS

LOCAL FRAMS

Local farms supply fresh, seasonal vegetables to nearby supermarkets. These farms often have direct partnerships with the stores, ensuring quick delivery and supporting the local economy. This approach provides customers with high-quality produce while reducing transportation time and costs.

WHOLESALE PRODUCE MARKETS

Wholesale produce markets aggregate vegetables from various growers, offering a wide range of options in large quantities. Supermarkets source from these markets to meet their diverse and consistent supply needs. Examples include large markets like the Hunts Point Produce Market in New York City.

AGRICULTURAL COOPERATIVES & DISTRIBUTION CENTER

Agricultural cooperatives are groups of farmers who combine their resources to market and distribute their produce more effectively. Distribution centers handle the logistics of storing and transporting vegetables from farms to supermarkets, ensuring fresh and efficiently delivered produce. Major grocery chains often operate their own distribution networks to streamline this process.
